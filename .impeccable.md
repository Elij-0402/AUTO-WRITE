## Design Context

### Users
InkForge serves the full spectrum of Chinese web novel authors:
- **Hobbyist serializers** cranking out chapters on 起点/晋江 late at night, chasing word counts and flow state
- **Professional serial authors** managing multiple novels, tracking world consistency across hundreds of chapters, publishing on deadline
- **Aspiring writers** using AI guidance and world-building tools to develop their craft

Common context: long sessions, often nocturnal, deep focus required. The tool must fade into the background during writing flow and surface power when needed.

### Brand Personality
**Atmospheric. Immersive. Literary.**

InkForge should feel like stepping into the world you're writing — a space that respects the gravity of storytelling. Not a productivity app that happens to handle text, but a writing environment that understands narrative craft. The UI itself should carry a sense of mood and texture, as if the workspace were an extension of the author's imagination.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Dark-primary, ink-stained, warm literary atmosphere. Think: a private study with low lamplight, aged paper, and the quiet weight of unwritten stories. Not gloomy — contemplative.
- **Palette evolution**: Move away from pure indigo-on-stone toward warmer, inkier tones. Sepia-touched ambers, deep ink-blacks with warm undertones, muted vermilion or cinnabar accents that echo traditional Chinese aesthetics (朱砂, 墨色, 宣纸) without becoming kitsch.
- **Theme**: Dark mode as the primary experience. Light mode supported but secondary.
- **Typography**: Chinese-first. Noto Sans SC as the body workhorse, but consider pairing with a more characterful display face for headings and UI chrome — something with literary weight.
- **References**: Scrivener/Obsidian for power-user depth and organizational muscle. But with far more atmospheric personality — these tools are functional, InkForge should also be evocative.

### Anti-References
- **No generic SaaS**: No Notion/Linear clinical minimalism. This is a creative tool, not a project manager.
- **No gamification**: No badges, streaks, cartoon mascots. Authors want to be taken seriously.
- **No IDE aesthetics**: No VS Code vibes. Writers aren't programmers — the interface should feel literary, not technical.
- **No AI dominance**: AI assists but shouldn't dominate. This is the author's workspace, not an AI playground. The chat panel is a quiet advisor, not the star of the show.

### Design Principles

1. **Ink over chrome** — Prioritize content and atmosphere over UI decoration. Every surface should feel like it recedes behind the writing, not competes with it.

2. **Warmth in the dark** — Dark mode doesn't mean cold. Warm neutrals, amber highlights, and subtle texture create a space that feels inhabited, not clinical.

3. **Quiet power** — Advanced features (world bible, AI context injection, outline tracking) should be discoverable through exploration, not shouted through the UI. Progressive disclosure over feature showcasing.

4. **Respect the craft** — Every design decision should honor the seriousness of long-form storytelling. Typography, spacing, and rhythm should mirror the reading experience authors are creating.

5. **Flow state guardian** — The interface must protect focus. No unnecessary motion, no attention-grabbing elements during writing. Transitions and feedback should be felt, not noticed.

### Accessibility
Best-effort approach: good contrast defaults, keyboard navigation where practical, respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Not targeting a specific WCAG level but maintaining sensible baseline quality.
